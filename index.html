Battle of the Yalu River

17 September 1894

Off the mouth of the Yalu, Japan's Combined Fleet — fast cruisers armed with quick-firing guns — met China's Beiyang Fleet and its two German ironclads, Dingyuan and Zhenyuan, whose 356 mm belts no Japanese shell could pierce. It became a contest between rate of fire and speed on one side, and heavy guns behind thick armour on the other. Take command and see whether history holds.
